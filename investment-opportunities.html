Why Invest in Real Estate

Indian property remains a core wealth-building asset — combining capital appreciation, rental income, and portfolio diversification.

Capital Growth

Metro-linked corridors in NCR, Pune, Hyderabad, and Bangalore have delivered strong long-term appreciation.

Passive Income

Quality apartments in IT hubs generate steady rental cash flow with professional tenant management.

Tangible Asset

Unlike volatile markets, real estate is a physical asset with intrinsic land value and utility.

Portfolio Balance

Diversify beyond equities and FDs — property hedges inflation over multi-year horizons.

RERA Oversight

Infrastructure Tailwinds

Metro, expressways, and airport expansions unlock new micro-markets ahead of price discovery.

Tax Benefits

Key income-tax provisions for home buyers and property investors under current Indian tax laws.

Section 80C

Principal Repayment

Deduction up to ₹ 1.5 lakh per year on home loan principal repayment (within overall 80C limit).

Section 24(b)

Interest Deduction

Up to ₹ 2 lakh per year on interest for self-occupied property; higher limits may apply for let-out property.

Section 80EEA

First-Time Buyers

Additional interest deduction for eligible first-time homebuyers in affordable housing (subject to conditions).

LTCG

Capital Gains

Long-term gains on property held 24+ months taxed at 12.5% (without indexation) post July 2024 reforms.

Section 54

Reinvestment Relief

Exemption on LTCG if proceeds are reinvested in another residential property within stipulated timelines.

TDS / NRI

Withholding Rules

Buyers must deduct TDS on property purchases above threshold; NRIs have specific TDS and DTAA considerations.

Tax rules change frequently. Consult a qualified CA for personalised advice.
