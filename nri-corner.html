Compliance

Taxation

Key tax considerations for NRIs owning residential property in India. Consult a chartered accountant for personalised advice.

TDS on Purchase

When buying from a resident seller, TDS @ 1% applies if property value exceeds ₹ 50 lakh (buyer deducts and deposits).

TDS on Sale

Buyer must deduct TDS @ 20% (+ surcharge) on sale consideration when purchasing from an NRI seller under Section 195.

Rental Income

Rental income is taxable in India. Standard deduction of 30% available. DTAA may provide relief depending on your country of residence.

Capital Gains

Long-term gains (held 24+ months) taxed at 12.5% without indexation (post Budget 2024). Exemptions available under Sections 54/54F.

Wealth Tax

Wealth tax abolished. However, property must be declared in Indian ITR if you qualify as RNOR or resident for tax purposes.

Global Reporting

Declare foreign assets in your country of residence as required (e.g. FATCA in USA, CRA in Canada). Keep Indian tax records for 7 years.

*Indicative information only. Tax laws change — please consult a qualified CA familiar with NRI taxation and DTAA provisions.

FEMA & remittance

Currency Guide

How to legally remit funds for property purchase and repatriate sale proceeds from India.

Permitted Accounts

NRIs must pay from NRE or NRO accounts. Payments from foreign accounts are converted via inward remittance to these accounts first.

Repatriation Rules

Sale proceeds up to the amount remitted from abroad (for that property) can be repatriated freely. Additional amounts subject to USD 1 million scheme per financial year.

Loan for NRIs

Indian banks offer home loans to NRIs against NRE/NRO income. EMI can be paid from overseas salary credited to NRE account.
